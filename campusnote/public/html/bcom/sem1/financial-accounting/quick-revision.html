Unit 1

Theoretical Framework & Accounting Process

Key Mnemonics & Concepts

🔑 Mnemonics

  • I-M-R-C — Identification → Measurement → Recording → Communication
  • B-I-T — Banks, Investors, Tax authorities (External Users)
  • C-U-T-V — Comparability, Understandability, Timeliness, Verifiability
  • G-C-A — Going Concern, Consistency, Accrual (AS-1)
  • D-D-P-P — Describe, Diagnose, Predict, Prescribe

📌 Qualitative Characteristics

  • Fundamental: Relevance (Materiality) + Faithful Representation
  • Enhancing (C-U-T-V): Comparability, Understandability, Timeliness, Verifiability

📌 GAAP – 12 Pillars

  • Entity, Money Measurement, Going Concern, Cost
  • Revenue Recognition, Realization, Accruals
  • Periodicity, Full Disclosure, Consistency
  • Materiality, Prudence (Conservatism)

📌 Provisions vs Contingencies

  • Provision: Probable (≥50%) + Reliable estimate → Recognized
  • Contingent Liability: Possible → NOT recognized (Disclose in Notes)
  • Contingent Asset: Probable → NOT recognized (Disclose in Notes)

📌 Change in Estimate vs Policy

  • Estimate: Prospective (forward only)
  • Policy: Retrospective (restate past)

📌 Ind AS Applicability

  • Listed: Ind AS (Mandatory)
  • Unlisted (Net Worth ≥ ₹250 Cr): Ind AS
  • Unlisted (Net Worth < ₹250 Cr): Traditional AS
🎯 Exam Tip: For a 15-mark essay on "Accounting as an Information System," use I-M-R-C framework and add a daily-life example (burger stall / sneaker store) for extra marks!
Unit 2

Business Income, PPE & Inventory Valuation

Formulas & Key Concepts

📌 R.G.I.N.

  • Revenue is Gross
  • Income is Net

📌 AS 9 – Revenue Recognition

  • Goods: Transfer of risks & rewards
  • Services: Percentage of Completion
  • Interest: Time proportion basis

📌 AS 10 – PPE

  • Cost = Purchase Price + Freight + Customs + Installation + Testing
  • Models: Cost Model OR Revaluation Model

📌 AS 26 – Intangibles

  • Acquired: Capitalized at cost
  • Research: Always expensed
  • Development: Capitalized (if criteria met)

📌 AS 2 – Inventory

  • Valuation = Lower of Cost or NRV
  • FIFO: Oldest stock sold first
  • Weighted Avg: New Avg = Total Cost ÷ Total Units

📌 Loss Types

  • Normal → Reduce quantity only
  • Abnormal → Remove quantity & cost → Charge to P&L
Depreciation Formulas SLM: (Cost – Scrap) × Rate %  |  WDV: Opening WDV × Rate %
💡 Exam Tip: If machine purchased before 1st April → Full year; after 1st October → Half year. Change in method → Recalculate from Day 1!
Unit 3

Final Accounts (Sole Proprietorship & NPOs)

Formulas & Adjustments Cheat Sheet
Core Formulas COGS = Opening Stock + Purchases + Direct Expenses – Closing Stock
Gross Profit = Sales – COGS
Net Profit = GP + Other Incomes – Other Expenses
Assets = Liabilities + Capital

Adjustments Cheat Sheet

AdjustmentP&L / TradingBalance Sheet
Closing StockCredit to TradingAsset
Outstanding ExpenseAdd to expenseLiability
Prepaid ExpenseSubtract from expenseAsset
DepreciationDebit P&LDeduct from Asset
Bad DebtsDebit P&LDeduct from Debtors
PDDDebit P&LDeduct from Debtors (after bad debts)
Discount on DebtorsDebit P&LDeduct from Debtors (after PDD)
⚡ Golden Rule: Every adjustment has TWO effects — one in P&L/Trading and one in Balance Sheet!

NPO – Quick Reference

📌 Subscription Formula

  • Actual Subscription =
  • Cash Received – Outstanding(beg) – Advance(end) + Outstanding(end) + Advance(beg)

📌 NPO Special Items

  • Life Membership: Capital → Add to Capital Fund
  • Specific Donation: Capital → Add to specific Fund
  • General Donation: Revenue → Credit to I&E A/c
Unit 4

Branches, Departments & Leases

Key Formulas & Rules

📌 Debtors System – Branch Account

  • Debit: Opening Stock, Goods sent, Expenses
  • Credit: Cash Sales, Cash from Debtors, Closing Stock, Closing Debtors

📌 Closing Debtors Formula

  • Opening + Credit Sales – Cash – Discount – Bad Debts = Closing Debtors

📌 Stock & Debtors System (Loading)

  • Cost + 25% → Loading = 1/5 of Invoice Price
  • Cost + 50% → Loading = 1/3 of Invoice Price

📌 4 Accounts to Prepare

  • Branch Stock A/c (at IP)
  • Branch Debtors A/c
  • Branch Adjustment A/c → Gross Profit
  • Branch P&L A/c → Net Profit

📌 Departmental – Allocation Bases

  • Rent, Lighting: Floor Area / Light Points
  • Salaries: Number of Employees
  • Depreciation: Value of Assets
  • Selling Expenses: Sales

📌 Lease Accounting (AS 19)

  • GIL = (Rental × Years) + GRV + UGRV
  • UFI = GIL – Fair Value
  • Net Investment = Fair Value – UGRV

Memory: GIL = Total money lessor gets; UFI = Profit/Interest part

Unit 5

Computerized Accounting Systems

Voucher Types, GST Rules & Security

📌 Voucher Types

  • Contra (F4): Cash/Bank transfers
  • Payment (F5): All payments
  • Receipt (F6): All receipts
  • Journal (F7): Adjustments, credit purchases of assets
  • Sales (F8): All sales
  • Purchase (F9): All purchases

📌 GST – ITC Utilization Order

  • Input IGST: IGST → CGST → SGST
  • Input CGST: CGST → IGST (NOT SGST)
  • Input SGST: SGST → IGST (NOT CGST)

📌 Reports Generated

  • Cash Book & Ledgers
  • Trial Balance (Live)
  • P&L Account
  • Balance Sheet
  • Cash Flow Statement

📌 3-2-1 Backup Strategy

  • 3 copies • 2 media • 1 off-site
🚀 Final Memory Trick — GST: "IGST can go anywhere (IGST → CGST → SGST). CGST can go to CGST or IGST. SGST can go to SGST or IGST. CGST and SGST can NEVER swap with each other!"

EXAM TIPS

Final Exam-Day Checklist

📌 Final Accounts

  • ✓ Every adjustment has TWO effects
  • ✓ PDD calculated AFTER bad debts
  • ✓ Discount on Debtors calculated AFTER PDD
  • ✓ Trial Balance items go to ONLY ONE place

📌 Branch Accounting

  • ✓ Opening items → Debit side
  • ✓ Goods sent & Expenses → Debit side
  • ✓ Sales & Cash from Debtors → Credit side
  • ✓ Closing Stock & Debtors → Credit side
  • ✓ Balancing = Profit (Credit bigger) or Loss (Debit bigger)

📌 GST

  • ✓ Input IGST → IGST → CGST → SGST
  • ✓ Input CGST → CGST → IGST (NOT SGST)
  • ✓ Input SGST → SGST → IGST (NOT CGST)
  • ✓ CGST and SGST can NEVER swap!
