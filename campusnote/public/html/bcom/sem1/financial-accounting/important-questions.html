1 Depreciation Accounting — Machinery Account Unit 2 Medium

On 1st April 2020, XYZ Ltd. purchased a machine for ₹6,00,000. Installation charges were ₹50,000.

On 1st October 2021, another machine was purchased for ₹3,50,000.

On 1st July 2022, the first machine was sold for ₹3,80,000.

On the same date (1st July 2022), a new machine was purchased for ₹2,80,000.

Depreciation is charged @ 10% p.a. on the Written Down Value (WDV) Method.

Accounts are closed on 31st March every year.

Required: Prepare the Machinery Account for 2020-21, 2021-22, and 2022-23.

Hint

Step 1: Calculate total cost of M1 = ₹6,00,000 + ₹50,000 = ₹6,50,000

Step 2: WDV Depreciation = Opening WDV × Rate %

Step 3: For sale, calculate depreciation up to date of sale only.

Step 4: Loss/Profit on sale = Sale Price – WDV on sale date.

WDV Dep = Opening WDV × 10%
2 Depreciation — Change in Method Unit 2 Hard

On 1st April 2019, a company purchased machinery for ₹8,00,000.

Depreciation was charged @ 15% p.a. on SLM for the first 2 years.

From 2021-22 onwards, the company decided to change the method to WDV @ 20% p.a.

Accounts are closed on 31st March every year.

Required: Calculate the depreciation for each year and show the adjustment entry for the change in method.

Hint

Step 1: Calculate SLM depreciation for 2019-20 and 2020-21.

Step 2: Recalculate depreciation under WDV from Day 1 (retrospective).

Step 3: Compare total depreciation charged (SLM) vs total under WDV.

Step 4: Shortfall = Debit to P&L; Excess = Credit to P&L in the year of change.

Shortfall = WDV Depreciation – SLM Depreciation (cumulative)
3 Inventory Valuation — FIFO & Weighted Average Unit 2 Medium

M/s. Rajesh Traders gives you the following details of material:

  • 01.01.2024: Opening Stock — 300 units @ ₹50 per unit
  • 10.01.2024: Purchases — 400 units @ ₹55 per unit
  • 15.01.2024: Sales — 350 units @ ₹80 per unit
  • 20.01.2024: Purchases — 500 units @ ₹60 per unit
  • 25.01.2024: Normal Loss — 20 units
  • 28.01.2024: Sales — 400 units @ ₹90 per unit
  • 30.01.2024: Purchases — 300 units @ ₹65 per unit

Required: Find Closing Stock and COGS using (1) FIFO and (2) Weighted Average (Perpetual).

Hint

FIFO: Oldest stock sold first. Closing stock from latest purchases.

Weighted Average: Recalculate average after EVERY purchase.

Normal Loss: Reduce quantity only (no cost impact).

Weighted Avg = Total Cost ÷ Total Units

7 Branch Accounting — Stock & Debtors System (with Loading) Unit 4 Hard

M/s. Sharma Traders has a HO in Mumbai and a Branch in Pune. Goods are invoiced to the branch at cost plus 50%.

Petty Expenses paid by Branch8,000
Closing Stock at IP1,50,000

Hint

9 Lease Accounting (AS 19) Unit 4 Easy

M/s. ABC Finance Ltd. leased a machine to M/s. XYZ Ltd. on the following terms:

ParticularsAmount (₹)

Lease Period5 Years
Annual Lease Rental (paid at year end)22,00,000
Guaranteed Residual Value (GRV)5,00,000
Unguaranteed Residual Value (UGRV)6,00,000

Required: Calculate (1) Gross Investment in Lease (GIL), (2) Unearned Finance Income (UFI), and (3) Net Investment in Lease.

Hint

10 GST — Order of Utilization of ITC Unit 5 Easy

M/s. Verma Traders has the following Input Tax Credit (ITC) available:

  • Input IGST: ₹40,000
  • Input CGST: ₹60,000
  • Input SGST: ₹50,000

The Output GST payable is as follows:

  • Outward IGST: ₹55,000
  • Outward CGST: ₹50,000
  • Outward SGST: ₹35,000

Required: Determine the order of utilization of ITC and prepare a statement showing the amount due, adjusted, and payable in cash.

Hint

Step 1: Input IGST → set off against Output IGST first.

Step 2: Input CGST → set off against Output CGST first, then IGST.

Step 3: Input SGST → set off against Output SGST first, then IGST.

Step 4: CGST and SGST can NEVER be set off against each other.

Cash Payable = Total Output GST – Total ITC Utilized
