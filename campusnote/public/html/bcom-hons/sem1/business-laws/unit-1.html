1. Introduction to Business Laws

Business Laws are the rules and legal principles that regulate business transactions, commercial relationships, contracts, rights, duties, liabilities, and remedies.

Simple Meaning

Business Law tells us how business agreements are created, how they are performed, and what happens when one party fails to follow the agreement.

Why Business Law is Important

  • It creates trust between parties in commercial transactions.
  • It protects buyers, sellers, consumers, and business owners.
  • It provides legal remedies if a contract is broken.
  • It helps businesses operate in a fair and lawful manner.

2. Indian Contract Act, 1872

The Indian Contract Act, 1872 is one of the most important business law statutes in India. It explains how legally enforceable agreements are created.

Term Meaning Exam Use
Agreement Every promise and every set of promises forming consideration for each other. Basic starting point of contract law.
Contract An agreement enforceable by law. Most important definition for exams.
Consideration Something given in return for a promise. Used to judge validity of contract.

Exam Focus

Remember this line: All contracts are agreements, but all agreements are not contracts. This is one of the most commonly asked concepts in Business Laws.

3. Essential Elements of a Valid Contract

  1. Offer and acceptance
  2. Lawful consideration
  3. Capacity of parties
  4. Free consent
  5. Lawful object
  6. Intention to create legal relationship
  7. Agreement not expressly declared void
  8. Certainty and possibility of performance

Quick Revision Trick

A valid contract needs a clear promise, legal exchange, capable parties, free consent, lawful purpose, and enforceability by law.

4. One-Line Revision

  • Business Law regulates commercial transactions.
  • An agreement becomes a contract only when it is enforceable by law.
  • Free consent and lawful object are essential for validity.
  • A void agreement has no legal effect.
  • A valid contract creates legal rights and obligations.
