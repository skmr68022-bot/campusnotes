🔹 Unit 1: Indian Contract Act, 1872

Contract Formula

Sec 2(h): Agreement + Legal Enforceability = Contract.
Sec 10 Essentials: Offer, Acceptance, Consideration, Capacity, Free Consent, Lawful Object, Legal Relations.

Consideration (Sec 2(d))

Rule: "No consideration, no contract."
Exceptions: Natural love/affection (written/registered), Past voluntary services, Time-barred debt (written), Agency, Gifts.

Capacity (Sec 11-12)

  • Minor: Agreement is Void ab initio (Case: Mohori Bibee v. Dharmodas Ghose).
  • Restitution: Minor must return goods only if they still possess them; no monetary compensation for consumed goods.
  • Sound Mind: Lunatics/insane persons cannot contract.

Free Consent (Vitiating Factors)

  • Coercion (Sec 15): Threatening with criminal force (Voidable).
  • Undue Influence (Sec 16): Dominating will (Voidable).
  • Fraud (Sec 17): Intentional deceit (Voidable).
  • Misrepresentation (Sec 18): Innocent falsehood (Voidable).
  • Mistake (Sec 20): Both parties misunderstand a material fact (Void).
Void Agreements (Sec 24-30)
  • Sec 27 (Restraint of Trade): Void. Exception: Sale of Goodwill (restricting trade within a specific local area/time).
  • Sec 28: Restraint of legal proceedings → Void.
  • Sec 30: Wagering agreements (Gambling) → Void.

Quasi-Contracts (Sec 68-72)

Obligations imposed by law to prevent unjust enrichment. Include: Supply of necessaries to minors (Sec 68), Finder of goods (Sec 71), Money paid by mistake (Sec 72).

🔹 Unit 2: Discharge & Remedies

Modes of Discharge

  • Performance: Natural completion.
  • Agreement (Sec 62): Novation (replace contract), Rescission (cancel), Alteration (change), Waiver (give up right), Merger.
  • Supervening Impossibility (Sec 56): Frustration. E.g., destruction of subject matter, war, death. (Case: Taylor v. Caldwell).
  • Breach: Failure to perform.

Breach of Contract

  • Actual: Occurs on due date.
  • Anticipatory: Occurs BEFORE due date. Victim can sue immediately. (Case: Hochster v. De La Tour).

Remedies (5 Types)

  • Rescission: Cancel the contract.
  • Damages (Sec 73-74):
    • Ordinary (Natural loss).
    • Special (Remoteness - need prior knowledge; Case: Hadley v. Baxendale).
    • Exemplary (Punitive - dishonor of cheque, marriage breach).
    • Nominal (No actual loss, just recognition).
    • Liquidated (Genuine estimate - allowed) vs Penalty (Exorbitant - not allowed, court gives reasonable).
  • Quantum Meruit (Sec 65): "As much as earned" - claim for work already done.
  • Specific Performance: Court orders actual execution (for unique goods/land).
  • Injunction: Court orders to STOP doing something.

Contract of Agency

  • Creation: Express, Implied, Estoppel (Holding out), Necessity (Emergency), Ratification (Approval later).
  • Termination: Revocation, Renunciation, Death/Insolvency, Completion of objective.
  • Sec 202: Agency "coupled with interest" is irrevocable.

🔹 Unit 4: Sale of Goods Act, 1930

Sale vs Agreement to Sell

BasisSale (Executed)Agreement to Sell (Executory)
OwnershipPasses immediatelyPasses in future
RiskOn BuyerOn Seller
RemedySue for PriceSue for Damages only

Conditions (Essential) vs Warranties (Collateral)

  • Condition: Breach → Right to reject goods + Damages.
  • Warranty: Breach → Right to claim Damages ONLY (cannot reject).
  • Implied Conditions (Sec 14): Title, Description, Fitness for purpose, Merchantable quality, Sale by sample.
Golden Rules for Unit 4
  • Caveat Emptor (Sec 16): "Buyer beware." Exceptions: Specific purpose + relying on seller, Fraud, Latent defects, Merchantable quality.
  • Nemo dat (Sec 27): "No one gives what he doesn't have." Exceptions: Mercantile agent, Co-owner, Voidable title, Unpaid seller's resale.
  • Unpaid Seller's rights (Sec 47-54): (Against goods) - Lien (retain possession), Stoppage in transit (stop goods with carrier), Resale (sell to 3rd party).

🔹 Unit 5: Limited Liability Partnership Act, 2008

Salient Features

  • Hybrid: Limited liability of Company + Flexibility of Partnership.
  • Separate Legal Entity: Owns assets, sues in its own name.
  • Limited Liability: Partners' personal assets are safe (except for own fraud).
  • Perpetual Succession: Death of a partner doesn't dissolve it.
  • Minimum Partners: 2 (No max limit). At least 2 must be Designated Partners (Individuals).

LLP v/s Partnership Firm

  • Registration: LLP is mandatory; Partnership is optional.
  • Liability: LLP is Limited; Partnership is Unlimited.

LLP Agreement (Sec 23) & Incorporation

  • Apply for Name (RUN-LLP) → File Form LLP-1 (Incorporation Doc) → Get Certificate of Incorporation → File LLP-3 (Agreement) within 30 days.
  • Designated Partners: Responsible for all statutory filings/compliances.
  • Whistleblowing (Sec 34): Partners/employees protected from harassment if they report fraud.
  • Conversion (Sec 55): Partnership Firm or Private Company can convert into LLP with consent.
