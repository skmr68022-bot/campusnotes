Unit 1: The Indian Contract Act, 1872

📌 6+6+6 Mark Questions (PYQs)
  • Q1. (a) Define the term "void agreements"? Discuss the agreements in restraint of trade with suitable examples.
    (b) Define the term "offer". What are the legal rules relating to a valid offer under Indian Contract Act, 1872?
    (c) Rahul, a minor, bought six luxury clothes from Raghav Traders on credit. Later on, after the refusal from Rahul to pay off the price, Mr. Raghav filed a suit against him. Can the sum be recovered from Mr. Rahul? Also discuss the minor's liability for necessaries.
  • Q2. Discuss the essential elements of a valid contract under Section 10 of the Indian Contract Act, 1872.
  • Q3. Define the term "Consideration". Discuss the exceptions to the rule "No consideration, no contract".
  • Q4. Explain the concept of "Free Consent". What are the various factors vitiating free consent under the Indian Contract Act?
📌 Additional Important Questions
  • Q5. Distinguish between "Void Agreement" and "Voidable Contract" with suitable examples.
  • Q6. What are Quasi-Contracts? Discuss the various types of Quasi-Contracts under Sections 68 to 72 of the Indian Contract Act, 1872.
  • Q7. Discuss the doctrines of "E-Commerce" and "Electronic Contracts" (Unit 1 Practical application).
  • Q8. Explain the legal rules regarding the "Capacity to Contract" (Minors, Persons of unsound mind, and persons disqualified by law).

Unit 2: Discharge of Contract & Remedies for Breach

📌 6+6+6 Mark Questions (PYQs)
  • Q1. (a) What do you mean by the term "discharge of contract"? Discuss the modes of discharge of contract by agreement under Indian Contract Act, 1872.
    (b) What do you mean by the term "breach of contract"? Differentiate between actual and anticipatory breach of contract.
    (c) Mr. Rahim, a renowned tailor for sherwanis, delivered a lot of clothes at Surat railway station for consignment to Lucknow on 1st November 2022. He needed this lot to be delivered by the end of November as the demand was at peak. He did not bring this to the notice of railways. Consignment was delayed by 1 month. Is Mr. Rahim entitled to claim the loss of profit?
  • Q2. (a) Discuss the concept of "Remedies for Breach of Contract" under the Indian Contract Act.
    (b) Distinguish between "Damages" and "Penalties" under Indian Contract Act, 1872.
    (c) Discuss "suit for Quantum Meruit" and "suit for Specific Performance" under the Indian Contract Act, 1872.
📌 Additional Important Questions
  • Q3. Explain the Doctrine of Supervening Impossibility (Frustration) under Section 56 of the Indian Contract Act. When does it apply?
  • Q4. Write a short note on the Rule of Remoteness of Damages (Hadley v. Baxendale).
  • Q5. What is the difference between "Liquidated Damages" and "Unliquidated Damages"?

Unit 3: Introduction to Special Contracts

📌 6+6+6 Mark Questions & Short Notes (PYQs)
  • Q1. (a) What is meant by "Agency by Estoppel" and "Agency by Necessity"? Explain the provisions of the Indian Contract Act relevant to the above under the Contract of Agency.
    (b) Differentiate between "sale" and "agreement to sell" under the Sales of Goods Act, 1930.
    (c) Define the terms "condition" and "warranties" under the Sales of Goods Act, 1930. Also discuss the implied conditions.
  • Q2. Write a short note on any three of the following (3 x 18 Marks format):
    (a) Contract of Indemnity v/s Contract of Guarantee.
    (b) Provisions related to Termination of Agency.
    (c) Void v/s Illegal agreements.
    (d) Distinguish between Bailment and Pledge.
📌 Additional Important Questions
  • Q3. Explain the rights and duties of a Bailee under the Contract of Bailment (Sec 151-153).
  • Q4. What is a Pledge? Discuss the rights of the Pawnee (Lender) when the Pawnor defaults (Sec 176).
  • Q5. How is a Contract of Agency created? Discuss the various modes of termination of an agency.

Unit 4: The Sale of Goods Act, 1930

📌 6+6+6 Mark Questions (PYQs)
  • Q1. (a) What is meant by "Caveat Emptor"? Explain the exceptions to the doctrine of caveat emptor under the Sales of Goods Act, 1930.
    (b) "A seller cannot transfer to the buyer of goods a title better than what he himself has." Comment, giving exceptions to the rule (Nemo Dat Quod Non Habet).
    (c) Discuss the rights of unpaid seller against the goods where property in goods has not transferred to the buyer.
  • Q2. (a) Differentiate between "sale" and "agreement to sell" under the Sales of Goods Act, 1930.
    (b) Distinguish between "Conditions" and "Warranties" in a contract of sale.
    (c) Explain the implied conditions under the Sale of Goods Act (Sec 14).
📌 Additional Important Questions
  • Q3. Discuss the rules regarding the transfer of ownership (property) in goods under Section 18 to 26 of the Sales of Goods Act.
  • Q4. What are the rights of an Unpaid Seller against the buyer personally (Suit for price and Suit for damages)?
  • Q5. Explain the rights of the buyer when the seller delivers a wrong quantity of goods (Sec 37).

Unit 5: The Limited Liability Partnership Act, 2008

📌 6+6+6 Mark Questions & Short Notes (PYQs)
  • Q1. (a) Who can become a partner in an LLP? What are the disqualifications for becoming a partner?
    (b) Explain the registration process of LLP under the LLP Act, 2008.
    (c) Discuss the provisions related to conversion of a firm into LLP under the LLP Act, 2008.
  • Q2. (a) Who are the designated Partners under the LLP Act, 2008? Also discuss the disqualifications for designated partners.
    (b) "Partner act as an agent of the LLP." Comment. Also discuss the liability of partners in case of fraud.
    (c) Differentiate between the partnership firm and LLP.
  • Q3. Write a short note on any three of the following (3 x 18 Marks format):
    (a) Whistle blowing under LLP Act, 2008.
    (b) Rules related to change of name of LLP firm.
    (c) LLP Agreement and Incorporation Document.
📌 Additional Important Questions
  • Q4. What are the salient features of a Limited Liability Partnership (LLP) under the LLP Act, 2008?
  • Q5. Compare and contrast the LLP and a Private Company (Company Act, 2013).
  • Q6. Explain the concept of "Small LLP" and the benefits it receives.

📝 Final Preparation Tip for DU Exams

  • Always map your answers: For 6+6+6 mark questions, the examiners strictly look for an Introduction (1 mark), Main Body with bullet points/tables (4 marks), and a Conclusion/Example (1 mark).
  • Use Landmark Cases: Don't just define concepts. Always pair them with cases like Mohori Bibee (Minors), Hadley v. Baxendale (Damages), and Hochster v. De La Tour (Anticipatory Breach) to secure full marks.
  • Comparison Tables: Whenever a question asks to "Distinguish" or "Compare" (like Sale vs Agreement, Indemnity vs Guarantee), always draw a 2-column or 3-column table. It saves time and fetches top marks.
  • Unit 5 is a fresh favorite: DU has been heavily testing LLP in recent years. Do not underestimate it—memorize the definitions of "Designated Partners" and "Whistleblowing" thoroughly.
