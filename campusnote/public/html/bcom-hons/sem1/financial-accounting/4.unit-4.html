1. Branch Accounting — Debtors System (Theory)

Imagine: You own a big showroom in Delhi (Head Office). You open a small branch in Jaipur. Your friend manages the Jaipur branch.

  • You (HO) send goods to the branch.
  • You (HO) pay all the big expenses like rent, salary, etc.
  • The branch just sells goods and collects money from customers.
  • All cash collected by the branch is sent to you (HO).

Now, at the end of the year, you want to know: Did the Jaipur branch make a profit or loss?
That's exactly what we find out using the Debtors System of Branch Accounting.

Head Office (HO)Main office that controls everything
BranchSmall outlet/shop in another city
Debtors SystemWe open just ONE "Branch Account" to find profit/loss
Branch AccountLike a personal account of the branch in HO books

Important Rules

  • Rule 1: Open a Branch Account in the Head Office ledger.
  • Rule 2: Debit Side — All items that represent money going TO the branch OR expenses paid FOR the branch.
  • Rule 3: Credit Side — All items that represent money coming FROM the branch OR what the branch still has (stock/debtors).
  • Rule 4: Balancing figure = Branch Profit (if Credit side is bigger) OR Branch Loss (if Debit side is bigger).

3. Branch Accounting — Stock & Debtors System with Loading (Theory)

Imagine: You own a big showroom in Delhi (Head Office). You send goods to your Jaipur branch. But here's the twist – you send goods at MRP (which is Cost + 50% profit for yourself).

Now, the branch sells these goods. At the end of the year, you want to know:

  • What is the actual cost of goods sold?
  • How much profit did the branch actually make?

But there's a problem – since you sent goods at MRP, the unsold stock at the branch has "unrealized profit" (fake profit) sitting in it. We need to remove that fake profit. That's exactly what the Stock & Debtors System does!

Invoice Price (IP)Price at which HO sends goods to branch (Cost + Loading)
Cost Price (CP)Actual cost of goods to HO
LoadingExtra profit added to cost (e.g., Cost + 25% → Loading = 25% of Cost)
Stock ReserveAmount of "fake profit" removed from closing stock
Branch Adjustment AccountAccount that removes loading from goods sent and closing stock to find Gross Profit
If HO sends atLoading =Loading as % of Invoice Price
Cost + 25%25% of Cost1/5 of Invoice Price
Cost + 50%50% of Cost1/3 of Invoice Price
Memory trick: If Cost + 25%, loading = 1/5 of IP. If Cost + 50%, loading = 1/3 of IP. Just remember these two – they come most often!

Four Accounts to Prepare

  1. Branch Stock Account — Records goods at Invoice Price (Debit = Opening Stock + Goods Sent; Credit = Sales + Returns + Closing Stock)
  2. Branch Debtors Account — Records credit sales and collections (Debit = Opening Debtors + Credit Sales; Credit = Cash received + Returns + Discount + Closing Debtors)
  3. Branch Adjustment Account — Removes loading from goods sent and closing stock to find Gross Profit
  4. Branch P&L Account — Takes Gross Profit and deducts expenses to find Net Profit
4. Stock & Debtors System — Numerical Problem

5. Departmental Accounting (Theory)

Imagine: You have a big store like Big Bazaar that sells:

  • Grocery items (Department A)
  • Clothing (Department B)
  • Electronics (Department C)

Each department has its own sales, purchases, and stock. But they share the same building (rent), same electricity bill, and same staff (salaries).

At the end of the year, you want to know:

  • Which department made the most profit?
  • How much did each department contribute to the overall profit?

That's exactly what Departmental Accounting does – it finds the profit/loss of each department separately!

DepartmentA separate section of the same business (like Grocery, Clothing, Electronics)
Direct ExpensesExpenses that belong to a specific department (like Wages of that department's workers)
Common ExpensesExpenses shared by all departments (like Rent, Electricity, Salaries)
Allocation BasisA fair way to split common expenses (like splitting rent based on floor area)
ExpenseAllocation Basis (How to Split)
Rent, Rates, Lighting, ElectricityFloor Area Occupied OR Light Points
Salaries, Labour WelfareNumber of Employees OR Time Devoted
Depreciation, InsuranceValue of Assets in each department
Selling Expenses, AdvertisementTotal Sales of each department
Power, FuelHorse Power / Machine Hours
Carriage InwardPurchases of each department
Memory trick: "Rent → Area; Salary → Employees; Depreciation → Assets; Selling → Sales"

7. Lease Accounting (AS 19) — Theory

Imagine: You want a car, but you don't have ₹10 lakhs to buy it outright. So you go to a company that says: "Pay us ₹2 lakhs every year for 5 years, and at the end, the car is yours (or you return it)."

This is exactly what a Finance Lease is – a long-term rental where the lessee (you) eventually gets the asset, and the lessor (company) earns interest/profit over time.

LessorThe company that owns the asset and gives it on lease
LesseeThe company that takes the asset on lease and pays rent
Fair ValueCurrent market price of the asset
Lease RentalAnnual payment made by lessee to lessor
GRVGuaranteed Residual Value – minimum amount lessee guarantees to pay at the end
UGRVUnguaranteed Residual Value – estimated value at the end NOT guaranteed by lessee
Gross Investment in Lease (GIL) GIL = (Annual Lease Rental × Number of Years) + GRV + UGRV
Unearned Finance Income (UFI) UFI = GIL – Fair Value

This is the total interest/profit the lessor will earn over the lease period.

Net Investment in Lease Net Investment = Fair Value – UGRV
8. Lease Accounting — Numerical Problem
PYQ-Style Question

M/s. ABC Finance Ltd. leased a machine to M/s. XYZ Ltd. on the following terms:

ParticularsAmount (₹)
Fair Value of the Machine80,00,000
Lease Period5 Years
Annual Lease Rental (paid at year end)18,00,000
Guaranteed Residual Value (GRV)4,00,000
Unguaranteed Residual Value (UGRV)5,00,000

Required: Calculate (1) Gross Investment in Lease (GIL), (2) Unearned Finance Income (UFI), and (3) Net Investment in Lease.

Step‑by‑step Solution
Step 1 · Calculate Gross Investment in Lease (GIL)
Formula: GIL = (Annual Rental × Years) + GRV + UGRV
= (18,00,000 × 5) + 4,00,000 + 5,00,000
= 90,00,000 + 4,00,000 + 5,00,000
GIL = ₹99,00,000
Step 2 · Calculate Unearned Finance Income (UFI)
Formula: UFI = GIL – Fair Value
= 99,00,000 – 80,00,000
UFI = ₹19,00,000

This ₹19,00,000 is the total interest/profit the lessor will earn over 5 years!

Step 3 · Calculate Net Investment in Lease
Formula: Net Investment = Fair Value – UGRV
= 80,00,000 – 5,00,000
Net Investment = ₹75,00,000
Gross Investment in Lease (GIL) ₹99,00,000
Unearned Finance Income (UFI) ₹19,00,000
Net Investment in Lease ₹75,00,000
📌 Quick Revision — Lease Accounting Exam Cheat Sheet:
• GIL = (Annual Rental × Years) + GRV + UGRV
• UFI = GIL – Fair Value
• Net Investment = Fair Value – UGRV
• Memory Trick: GIL = TOTAL money lessor will get. UFI = PROFIT/INTEREST part.
