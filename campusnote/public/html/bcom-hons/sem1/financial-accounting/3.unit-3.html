1. Concept of Final Accounts

Imagine: You run a small kirana (grocery) shop. At the end of the year, you want to know:

  • How much profit did I make from buying and selling goods? → Trading Account
  • What was my total net income after paying all other expenses? → Profit & Loss Account
  • What do I own and what do I owe? → Balance Sheet
Trading AccountShows Gross Profit (Sales – Cost of Goods Sold)
P&L AccountShows Net Profit (after all expenses & incomes)
Balance SheetShows financial position (Assets = Liabilities + Capital)
2. Important Formulas (Must Memorize)
Cost of Goods Sold (COGS) Opening Stock + Purchases + Direct Expenses – Closing Stock
Gross Profit Sales – COGS
Net Profit Gross Profit + Other Incomes – Other Expenses
Balance Sheet Assets = Liabilities + Capital
3. Trading Account

The Trading Account is the first fundamental component of Final Accounts. Its primary purpose is to ascertain the Gross Profit or Gross Loss incurred purely from the core trading activities.

The Core Logic

It operates on the principle of comparing the Net Sales Revenue against the Cost of Goods Sold (COGS). It strictly ignores any administrative or financial expenses.

COGS Formula

COGS = Opening Stock + Net Purchases + Direct Expenses - Closing Stock

Nature of Account

It is a Nominal Account. Therefore, the rule applies: Debit all direct expenses and losses, Credit all direct incomes and gains.

Particulars (Debit Side - Direct Costs)Particulars (Credit Side - Direct Incomes)
To Opening Stock: The raw materials, WIP, and finished goods from the previous year.By Sales: Gross sales made during the year.
To Purchases: (Less: Purchase Returns / Returns Outward)By Sales Returns: (Less: Returns Inward) - Deducted from Gross Sales.
To Direct Wages: Paid to workers directly involved in manufacturing/unloading.By Closing Stock: Valued at Cost or Net Realizable Value, whichever is lower (AS-2).
To Carriage / Freight Inwards: Transport costs to bring goods into the factory.
To Manufacturing Expenses: Factory power, coal, gas, water, and factory rent.By Gross Loss (Balancing Figure): Transferred to P&L Debit side.
To Gross Profit (Balancing Figure): Transferred to P&L Credit side.
4. Profit & Loss Account

The P&L Account takes the Gross Profit and deducts all Indirect Expenses to calculate the actual survivability of the business: the Net Profit.

Indirect Expenses (Debit Side)

  • Office & Administrative Expenses: Office Rent, Salaries, Printing & Stationery, Telephone, Audit fees.
  • Selling & Distribution Expenses: Advertising, Salesman Commission, Carriage Outwards, Godown Rent.
  • Financial Expenses & Losses: Interest on loans, Bank charges, Discount allowed, Bad debts, Depreciation.

Indirect Incomes (Credit Side)

  • By Gross Profit b/d: Brought down from the Trading Account.
  • Operating Non-Sales Income: Discount received, Commission received, Rent received.
  • Non-Operating Income: Interest on investments, Dividend received, Profit on sale of fixed assets.
5. Adjustments Cheat Sheet
⚡ THE GOLDEN RULE OF ADJUSTMENTS: Every adjustment given outside the Trial Balance MUST have TWO effects (one Debit, one Credit) to satisfy the accounting equation. If it only has one, your Balance Sheet will fail to tally.
AdjustmentTreatment in P&L / TradingTreatment in Balance Sheet
Closing StockCredit to Trading A/cShow as Asset (Current)
Outstanding ExpenseAdd to that expenseShow as Liability
Prepaid ExpenseSubtract from that expenseShow as Asset
Accrued IncomeAdd to that incomeShow as Asset
Income Received in AdvanceSubtract from that incomeShow as Liability
DepreciationDebit P&L (charge expense)Deduct from Asset value
Bad DebtsDebit P&LDeduct from Debtors
Provision for Doubtful Debts (PDD)Debit P&LDeduct from Debtors (after bad debts)
Provision for Discount on DebtorsDebit P&LDeduct from Debtors (after PDD)
Goods given as Free SamplesDeduct from Purchases; Debit P&L as AdvertisementNot applicable
Goods taken by Owner (Drawings)Deduct from PurchasesDeduct from Capital
6. GST Treatment in Final Accounts
EntryTreatment
Input CGST / SGST / IGSTShown as Asset (Current Asset) in Balance Sheet
Output CGST / SGST / IGSTShown as Liability (Current Liability) in Balance Sheet
Input IGST set-offFirst set off against Output IGST, then CGST, then SGST
Input CGST set-offSet off against Output CGST, then IGST
Input SGST set-offSet off against Output SGST, then IGST
Exam tip: For GST, always remember the order: IGST → CGST → SGST. Show Input GST as Asset and Output GST as Liability.

📌 Quick Revision — Final Accounts Exam Cheat Sheet:
• For PDD, calculate AFTER writing off bad debts.
• For Discount on Debtors, calculate AFTER PDD.
• Every adjustment has TWO effects — one in P&L/Trading and one in Balance Sheet.
• Trial Balance items go to ONLY ONE place (Trading, P&L, or Balance Sheet).
• Closing Stock is always credited to Trading and shown as Current Asset.

8. Not-for-Profit Organisations (NPOs) — Theory

Imagine: Your colony has a Resident Welfare Association (RWA). Every year, they collect money from members (subscriptions), spend it on maintenance, and at the end of the year, they want to know:

  • How much did we collect and spend? → Receipt & Payment Account
  • What was our actual surplus/deficit? → Income & Expenditure Account
  • What do we own and owe? → Balance Sheet
Receipt & Payment A/cSummary of ALL cash/bank transactions (cash basis)
Income & Expenditure A/cLike P&L; prepared on accrual basis (only revenue items)
Capital FundLike Capital of a business — what the club owns minus what it owes
SubscriptionMain income of any club — annual fees paid by members
Life Membership FeeOne-time payment — capitalized (not shown as income)
ItemTreatment in I&E A/cTreatment in Balance Sheet
SubscriptionShow actual income (after adjustments)Outstanding = Asset; Advance = Liability
Outstanding ExpensesAdd to expenseLiability
Prepaid ExpensesSubtract from expenseAsset
DepreciationDebit I&EDeduct from Asset
Life Membership Fees❌ NOT shownAdd to Capital Fund
Specific Donations (Prize Fund, Building Fund)❌ NOT shownAdd to that specific Fund
Sale of Old Asset❌ NOT shownAdd to Capital Fund (less book value)
Subscription Formula (Exam Cheat Sheet) Cash Received – Outstanding (begin) – Advance (end) + Outstanding (end) + Advance (begin)

Memory trick: "Current year ka actual income = Jo mila + jo aaya (end) – jo tha (start) – jo aage ka mila + jo pehle ka tha"

📌 Quick Revision — NPO Exam Cheat Sheet:
• Life Membership Fees → Add to Capital Fund (NOT income)
• Specific Donations → Add to that specific Fund (NOT income)
• Subscription → Use the adjustment formula (don't take cash received directly)
• Stationery → Calculate consumed = Opening + Purchases – Closing
• Depreciation → Debit I&E; Deduct from Asset in B/S
