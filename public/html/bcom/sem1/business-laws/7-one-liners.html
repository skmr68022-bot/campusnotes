📌 Unit 1: Indian Contract Act, 1872
  • Sec 2(h): "An agreement enforceable by law is a contract." (Contract = Agreement + Enforceability).
  • Sec 10 Essentials: Offer, Acceptance, Consideration, Capacity, Free Consent, Lawful Object, Legal Relations.
  • Sec 2(d) Consideration: "Something in return" (Quid pro quo). Core Rule: "No consideration, no contract."
  • Exceptions to No Consideration (Sec 25): Natural love/affection (written/registered), Past voluntary services, Time-barred debt (written), Agency, Gifts.
  • Minor's Capacity (Sec 11): Agreement with minor is void ab initio (Mohori Bibee v. Dharmodas Ghose).
  • Restitution for Minor: If minor fraudulently obtains goods, they must return goods only if still in possession. No monetary compensation for consumed goods.
  • Free Consent (Vitiating Factors): Coercion (Voidable), Undue Influence (Voidable), Fraud (Voidable), Misrepresentation (Voidable), Mistake (Void if Bilateral).
  • Sec 27 (Restraint of Trade): Void. Exception: Sale of Goodwill (reasonable local restraint is allowed).
  • Other Void Agreements: Restraint of Marriage (Sec 26), Restraint of Legal Proceedings (Sec 28), Wagering/Gambling (Sec 30).
  • Quasi-Contracts (Sec 68-72): Obligations imposed by law to prevent unjust enrichment. (E.g., Sec 71: Finder of goods; Sec 72: Money paid by mistake).
📌 Unit 2: Discharge & Remedies
  • Modes of Discharge: Performance, Agreement (Novation, Rescission, Alteration, Waiver, Merger), Impossibility (Frustration), Breach, Law, Lapse of time.
  • Sec 56 - Supervening Impossibility: Contract becomes impossible after creation (e.g., war, destruction of subject). Taylor v. Caldwell.
  • Actual Breach: Non-performance on the exact due date.
  • Anticipatory Breach (Sec 39): Repudiation BEFORE the due date. Victim can sue immediately (Hochster v. De La Tour).
  • Damages - Ordinary: Direct, natural loss arising from the breach.
  • Damages - Special: Remote/Consequential losses. Only claimable if the breaching party had prior knowledge (Hadley v. Baxendale - Mr. Rahim case).
  • Damages - Liquidated vs Penalty: Liquidated = Genuine estimate (Allowed). Penalty = Exorbitant intimidation (Disallowed by court under Sec 74).
  • Quantum Meruit (Sec 65): "As much as earned". Claim for payment for work already done in a void/breached contract.
  • Specific Performance: Court order forcing actual execution of the promise (used for unique land/goods).
  • Injunction: Court order forcing a party to STOP doing something illegal/harmful.
📌 Unit 3: Special Contracts
  • Indemnity (Sec 124): 2 Parties (Indemnifier, Holder). Liability is Primary (Immediate compensation). Example: Insurance.
  • Guarantee (Sec 126): 3 Parties (Surety, Creditor, Principal Debtor). Liability is Secondary (Surety pays only if Debtor defaults). Example: Bank loans with co-signer.
  • Bailment (Sec 148): Delivery of goods for safekeeping/repair (Bailor & Bailee). Bailee must take reasonable care.
  • Pledge (Sec 172): Special bailment where goods are given as security for a debt (Pawnor & Pawnee).
  • Right of Pawnee: If debtor defaults, Pawnee can sell the goods after giving reasonable notice to the Pawnor.
  • Agency (Sec 182): Agent acts on behalf of Principal. No consideration required to create an agency.
  • Creation of Agency: Express, Implied, Estoppel (Holding out), Necessity (Emergency), Ratification.
  • Termination of Agency: Revocation by Principal, Renunciation by Agent, Death/Insolvency of either, Completion of business.
  • Sec 202 - Irrevocable Agency: If agency is "coupled with interest" (agent has a personal share in the profit), it cannot be revoked.
📌 Unit 4: Sale of Goods Act, 1930
  • Sale (Sec 4(3)): Immediate transfer of ownership (Executed). Risk is on Buyer. Remedy = sue for price.
  • Agreement to Sell (Sec 4(4)): Future transfer of ownership (Executory). Risk is on Seller. Remedy = sue for damages.
  • Condition (Sec 12(2)): Essential stipulation. Breach = Right to reject goods + claim damages.
  • Warranty (Sec 12(3)): Collateral stipulation. Breach = Right to claim damages only (cannot reject goods).
  • Implied Conditions (Sec 14): Title, Description, Fitness for purpose, Merchantable quality, Sale by sample.
  • Caveat Emptor (Sec 16): "Let the buyer beware". Exceptions: Specific purpose + reliance on seller, Fraud, Latent defects, Unmerchantable quality.
  • Nemo dat (Sec 27): "No one can give what they don't have." Seller cannot transfer better title than their own.
  • Exceptions to Nemo dat: Sale by Mercantile agent, Sale by Co-owner, Sale under Voidable title, Sale by Unpaid seller (resale).
  • Unpaid Seller (Sec 45): Seller who hasn't received full payment.
  • Rights of Unpaid Seller AGAINST GOODS: Lien (Retain possession), Stoppage in Transit (Stop goods with carrier), Resale (Sell to a third party).
📌 Unit 5: LLP Act, 2008
  • LLP Core Features: Hybrid structure (Limited liability of Company + Flexibility of Partnership).
  • Separate Legal Entity: LLP owns assets, sues/gets sued in its own name.
  • Partner Liability: Limited. Personal assets are safe (except for personal fraud).
  • Perpetual Succession: Death/retirement of a partner does not dissolve the LLP.
  • Partners: Minimum 2, No maximum limit.
  • Designated Partners: Every LLP must have at least 2 individuals as DPs. They are responsible for all statutory compliance.
  • LLP vs Traditional Partnership: LLP = Mandatory registration & Limited liability. Partnership = Optional registration & Unlimited liability.
  • LLP Agreement (Sec 23): Written contract governing mutual rights, duties, and profit-sharing among partners.
  • Incorporation Process: Apply Name (RUN-LLP) → File Form LLP-1 → Get Certificate of Incorporation → File LLP-3 (Agreement) within 30 days.
  • Whistleblowing (Sec 34): Partners/Employees protected from harassment/demotion for reporting fraud/mismanagement.
  • Conversion (Sec 55): Traditional Partnership Firm or Private Company can convert into an LLP with proper consent.
