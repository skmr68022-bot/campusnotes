How to Use These Numerical Notes

This file is designed for numerical-heavy Delhi University subjects such as Financial Accounting, Cost Accounting, Corporate Accounting, Statistics, Economics, Business Mathematics, Finance, and other calculation-based papers.

Best Use

First revise the formulas and formats, then study solved examples step by step, and finally attempt practice questions without looking at the solution.

Exam Strategy

In numerical questions, marks are usually awarded for correct format, working notes, steps, formula, and final answer. Do not write only the final answer.

6. How to Present Numerical Answers in DU Exams

Presentation matters in numerical papers. Even if the final answer is wrong, correct steps and working notes can help students get marks.

Part of Answer What to Write Why It Matters
Formula / Format Write formula or draw correct format first. Shows correct method.
Working Notes Show calculations separately. Helps examiner give step marks.
Final Answer Write final result clearly. Makes answer complete.
Units / Amounts Mention ₹, %, ratio, units, or relevant label. Avoids confusion.

7. Common Mistakes in Numerical Questions

  • Writing only final answer without steps.
  • Not preparing working notes for adjustment-based questions.
  • Using wrong formula or wrong format.
  • Ignoring units, dates, rates, percentages, or amount labels.
  • Making calculation mistakes due to hurry.
  • Not checking whether answer is reasonable.
  • Mixing debit/credit, plus/minus, or opening/closing values.

Mistake Avoidance Tip

After solving, check formula, values, signs, units, and final answer once before moving to the next question.
