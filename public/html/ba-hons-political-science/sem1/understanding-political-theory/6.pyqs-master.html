🔗 Cross-Unit / Interdisciplinary Questions

These questions require you to draw connections across multiple units. They test your ability to synthesise information.

  • 2019 (20 marks): "What is politics? Discuss with reference to theorizing the political." — Connects Unit 1 (definition of politics) with Unit 3 (traditions) and Unit 4 (critical perspectives).
    Integrate thinkers from Unit 1 (Weber, Arendt) with Unit 3 (Liberalism/Marxism) to show different ways of defining politics.
  • 2022 (15 marks): "How do different traditions of political theory view the state and its legitimacy?" — Connects Unit 3 (traditions) with Unit 5 (legitimacy and obligation).
    Compare Liberal (Locke: limited, consent-based), Marxist (state as coercive tool), Anarchist (state is illegitimate), and Conservative (state as organic) views.
  • 2021 (12 marks): "To what extent do critical perspectives (feminism and postmodernism) challenge the mainstream traditions of political theory?" — Connects Unit 3 (mainstream) with Unit 4 (critical).
    Show how feminism critiques the patriarchal bias of liberalism, and how postmodernism critiques the grand narratives of liberalism and Marxism.

Integrative framework for these questions: Start by defining the core concepts (e.g., politics, state). Then present the mainstream views (Liberal, Marxist, Conservative). Then introduce the critical perspectives (Feminist, Postmodern) and discuss how they challenge or complicate the mainstream. Conclude by acknowledging that no single tradition has a monopoly on truth — a pluralistic understanding is necessary.
